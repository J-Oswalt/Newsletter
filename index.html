Family Assessment Newsletter

🧠 Understanding Assessments: What Families Need to Know

In our classroom, we use different types of assessments to better understand your child’s needs and celebrate their growth:

  • Diagnostic Assessments (at the start): Know what your child already understands and where we begin instruction.
  • Formative Assessments (during learning): Small checks (activities, observations, or quizzes) guide teaching day-by-day.
  • Summative Assessments (at the end): Show what your child has learned, like unit tests or projects.

📚 How We Align Assessments with Standards

Our assessments meet English Language Proficiency (ELP) Standards and content area standards (math, science, reading) to support English growth and subject learning together.

🧩 How Assessment Guides Teaching

Assessment results help us:

  • Choose better words, visuals, and examples.
  • Plan activities that fit student levels.
  • Offer extra help (intervention) or extra challenges (enrichment).

📊 State Assessments and Student Progress

State tests measure English skills and subject knowledge. We use results to track progress and plan future support.

⚖️ Accommodations for English Learners

We ensure fairness for ELs by offering:

  • Extra time
  • Simpler English instructions
  • Bilingual dictionaries
  • Read-aloud directions

🪞 Helping Students Reflect on Their Learning

We help students think about their progress by:

  1. Exit Tickets — Students write or draw one thing they learned.
  2. Self-Checklists — Students check off skills they feel confident about.

💬 Communication is Key!

How we stay connected:

  • Weekly Updates — Newsletters and class apps (translated when possible!)
  • Parent-Teacher Conferences — Interpreters provided if needed.
  • Celebration Notes — Positive notes about achievements!
